W.E.B. Du Bois Library Falcon Cam

Fun Facts and History

Have you ever been curious about the history of the Pelegrine falcons that live on the top of W.E.B. Du Bois Library? Well luckily since 2012, UMass has set up a falcon cam for the university to view these birds up close.

A Brief Overview

  • Peregrine falcons have successfully nested on the roof of the Du Bois Library at UMass Amherst since 2003. The most well known pair, which nested on the Library roof from 2003-2014, hatched a total of 37 chicks!
  • A live web camera was installed atop the Du Bois Library in 2012, allowing the public to watch the pair raise their chicks, and garnering more than 200,000 viewers in 2014-double the audience in 2012.

Fun Facts!

  1. The Peregrine Falcon chicks were bred, raised and released by the Peregrine Fund, a non-profit organization created in 1970 dedicated to saving birds of prey from extinction.
  2. Peregrine Falcons are one of the most widely distributed birds, living in every continent except Antarctica.
  3. Peregrine Falcons are known to be one of the fastest animals on the planet!
