What is Accessibility?

Digital accessibility guarantees that all individuals, regardless of ability, can perceive, comprehend, navigate, and engage with online content. Accessibility means that everyone is afforded the opportunity to access the same information, providing users with equally integrative and equally effective information that doesn’t exclude any group or person. By being attentive towards accessibility, you remove barriers that previously excluded/prevented access for people on the web(Indiana University, Understanding accessibility).

Although it is often thought that accessibility only applies to one group of people; one in four adults in the U.S. have some type of disability. This statistic shows that accessibility is a diability rights and social justice issue. For digital accessibility in particular, the design goal should be to make electronic content available to and usable by everyone, at the same time, with the same ease of use, regardless if they have a disability or not.

With new laws today, it is also a legal requirement in many contries to make spaces and places accessible. Some of these important laws include; the Architectural Barriers Act of 1968, the Rehabilitation Act of 1973, Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, Landmark Legislation, which includes ADA, and most recently Section 508. The act that focuses on digital accessibility is Section 508 of the Landmark Legislation Act of 1998 which came after Section 504. This law ensures access to electronic forms of information and information technology created by Federal agencies.

Finally, another important aspect of digital accessibility is WCAG which stands for Web Content Accessibility Guidelines. These guidelines come from the World Wide Web Consortium(W3C), which is the international standards organization that sets the standards for HTML/CSS. These sets of recommendations were created to make web content more accessible, not only for people with disabilities, but also for everyone. There are different levels of conformance within WCAG ranging from A to AAA, AAA being the most aspirational level of accessibility. These laws and guidelines however only work if they are enforced. It should also be noted that a checklist can only be so effective, but human experience and real stories create real impact within accessibility(May).
